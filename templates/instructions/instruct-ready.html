Instructions

In this experiment, you will play the role of a research laboratory technician examining classifications of gemstones.

We will ask you to classify eight specimens.

There can be multiple true classifications for each specimen.

You can make your judgments of the classifications based on two sources of information. One source of information is the laboratory tests you run yourself. The second source of information is judgments from previous technicians.

Each test you run yourself will indicate a selection of possible classifications the specimen. For example, one test might indicate that a specimen is likely one of either the "pesho", "ivil", or "thyun" classifications, which are all names of potential classifications. The true classifications have a 60% chance of producing positive evidence in each test, while each incorrect classification has a 30% chance of producing positive evidence. Usually about half of the classifications will be correct and about half will be incorrect.

As for the second source of information you will have, the set of technicians working on this project (i.e., previous MTurk workers) operate in shifts. You will be able to observe the judgment of workers from the previous shift as long as you are not in the first shift. For example, you might see that a technician from the previous round chose "pesho" and "ivil" as their judged classifications.

You will receive a bonus based on your performance at classification.
